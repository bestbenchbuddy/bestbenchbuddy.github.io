Green Bean
Rejection System

The problem statement: A company called "Matt and Steve's Extreme Bean" was looking to automate their heavily manual process of creating picked green bean jars. The goal was to create an automated system that could determine whether a green bean was good or bad.

Criteria for a bad bean would either be mold, dirt, or broken.





The system created used a video camera to watch a conveyorbelt while the beans came down, and determine whether it was a good bean, bad bean.
This was done using Tensorflows neural network to create an image classifier. By taking pictures of good and bad green beans (as well as pictures of the conveyorbelt with part of the green bean showing on the camera) The system could determine with an accuracy of >95% whether the bean it was looking at should be rejected or not.

In order to train the neural network, we used 200 photos of good and bad beans, as well as 50 or so pictures of beans not entirely in frame for the camera.
